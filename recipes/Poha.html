Poha

Description

Poha, also known as flattened rice,

originates from the Indian subcontinent.

Parboiled before flattening,

it requires very little to no cooking.

Enjoyed under various names like pauwa, sira, chira, and chivda.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups thick Poha (rice flakes)
  • 1 medium Onion, finely chopped (approx. 1/3 cup)
  • 1 small Potato, peeled and finely chopped (approx. ½ cup)
  • 1/3 teaspoon Mustard Seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon Cumin Seeds
  • 8-10 Curry Leaves
  • 1 Green Chili, finely chopped
  • 1 pinch of Asafoetida (Hing), optional
  • 1½ tablespoon Peanuts (ground nuts)
  • 1/4 teaspoon Turmeric Powder
  • 1½ teaspoons Lemon Juice
  • 1/2 tablespoon Sugar
  • 1 tablespoon grated Coconut, optional
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons Oil
  • 1/4 cup Pomegranate Seeds, optional
  • 2 tablespoons finely chopped Coriander Leaves

Steps

  1. Step 1: Rinse 2 cups of thick Poha (rice flakes) in water until they soften. Drain the water and set the Poha aside.

  2. Step 2: In a large pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil over medium heat.

  3. Step 3: Add 1/3 teaspoon of mustard seeds and 1/2 teaspoon of cumin seeds. Let them splutter.

  4. Step 4: Add 8-10 curry leaves and a finely chopped green chili. Stir for a minute.

  5. Step 5: Add a pinch of asafoetida (Hing), optional.

  6. Step 6: Add 1 medium finely chopped onion and sauté until it becomes translucent.

  7. Step 7: Add 1 small potato (peeled and finely chopped). Cook for a few minutes until the potato softens.

  8. Step 8: Add 1½ tablespoon of peanuts (ground nuts) and cook for a minute.

  9. Step 9: Sprinkle 1/4 teaspoon of turmeric powder over the mixture and mix well.

  10. Step 10: Add the soaked Poha to the pan. Mix everything together gently.

  11. Step 11: Squeeze 1½ teaspoons of lemon juice over the Poha.

  12. Step 12: Add 1/2 tablespoon of sugar and salt to taste. Mix well.

  13. Step 13: Cover the pan and let the Poha cook on low heat for about 5 minutes.

  14. Step 14: Uncover and give it a final stir.

  15. Step 15: Optional: Garnish with grated coconut, pomegranate seeds, and finely chopped coriander leaves.
