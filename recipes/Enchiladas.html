Vegetarian Enchiladas

Description

Vegetarian Enchiladas are a delicious Mexican dish that features rolled tortillas filled with a savory blend of vegetables, beans, and cheese, smothered in a flavorful enchilada sauce, and baked to perfection. This meatless version is perfect for vegetarians and anyone who loves a tasty, satisfying meal!

Ingredients

  • 6-8 large flour tortillas
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh, frozen, or canned)
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup enchilada sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar or Mexican cheese blend
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped, for garnish

Steps

  1. Step 1: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Step 2: In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add diced onion and cook until softened.

  3. Step 3: Add minced garlic and diced red bell pepper to the skillet. Sauté for a couple of minutes until the pepper softens.

  4. Step 4: Stir in the drained black beans, corn kernels, ground cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Cook for another 2-3 minutes to combine the flavors. Remove the skillet from heat.

  5. Step 5: Pour about 1/2 cup of enchilada sauce into the bottom of a baking dish.

  6. Step 6: To assemble the enchiladas, place a tortilla on a clean surface. Spoon a generous amount of the vegetable mixture onto the center of the tortilla, and sprinkle some shredded cheese on top.

  7. Step 7: Roll up the tortilla and place it seam side down in the baking dish. Repeat with the remaining tortillas and filling.

  8. Step 8: Pour the remaining enchilada sauce over the rolled tortillas, making sure they are fully covered.

  9. Step 9: Sprinkle the remaining shredded cheese on top of the enchiladas.

  10. Step 10: Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for about 20 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

  11. Step 11: Remove the foil and bake for an additional 5-10 minutes to lightly brown the cheese.

  12. Step 12: Garnish with chopped fresh cilantro before serving.

  13. Step 13: Serve the delicious Vegetarian Enchiladas with your favorite toppings like sour cream, guacamole, or salsa.
